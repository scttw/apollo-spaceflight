02 Rocketdyne F-1 — 1.5 million pounds of thrust

Five of these, side by side

At the very bottom of the Saturn V sat five of these — the F‑1 engines. Together they did the hardest job of all: lifting the enormous rocket up off the ground.

Each engine gulped tonnes of fuel every single second and turned it into a roaring column of fire. They only had to burn for a few minutes — but those few minutes are what pushed Apollo off the planet and sent it on its way to the Moon.
