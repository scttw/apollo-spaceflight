03 Earthrise — 240,000 miles from home

Two worlds

Once the engines went quiet, the spacecraft coasted across space — about 240,000 miles — all the way to the Moon. In 1969, the first astronauts climbed down and stepped onto its dusty grey surface.

From far out there, they turned around and saw home: Earth, a tiny blue marble hanging in the black. These are the two worlds at the heart of the Apollo story — the planet we left, and the Moon we reached.
